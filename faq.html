General & Technical Questions

X-ray gauging is a non-contact method to measure the thickness of materials like steel and aluminum. It uses a C-frame with an x-ray source in the lower arm and a detector in the upper arm. By measuring how the material absorbs the x-rays, we can determine its thickness with high accuracy and speed.

While other manufacturers regulate beam intensity, RSI also tightly regulates beam energy. This ensures excellent long-term stability and maintains accuracy without needing a large number of internal samples. Our proprietary X-ray Spectrum Correction (XSC) technique compensates for the "aging" effect of components, guaranteeing that alloy correction factors do not change over the life of the gauge.
